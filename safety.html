Safety Measures

Don't be unsure, be secure!

Detect and remove malware

Having knowledge of how some of the different malwares work can be of great help in identifying the type of malware (National Cybersecurity Alliance, 2023).
By detecting and removing malware, you can maintain the security of your computer system. Not everyone knows how to do this in most cases.
Here are some tips:

1

Install antivirus software from a well-known vendor such as Norton, Avast or McAfee. These services deliver software with different types of functions and degrees of security. It may be a good idea to do a little research on which type of supplier meets your needs. The price varies and most have a free trial period so that you can test the programs. Of the antivirus programs we have attached as an example, Avast is free (Windows defender is also free and comes with Microsoft Windows computers as a standard).

Here is a link to antivirus programs provided by the aforementioned suppliers:
Norton - https://no.norton.com
Avast - https://www.avast.com
McAfee - https://www.mcafee.com

2

Always keep antivirus software updated to its latest version. The suppliers of antivirus software will constantly refine and improve their program. It may be that someone has found a hole in the program that they want to exploit. the supplier will fix this as quickly as possible to rectify the weakness. This is one of the important reasons why you should always take care to keep the program up to date.

3

A good way to detect malware is to take a full scan of the computer using an antivirus program. This function can take some time, but in return you can get information on whether you have files or programs with malicious code.



4

To be extra safe, you can also download and use programs such as Malwarebytes and spybot search. These programs are of the anti-malware software type and can also be used to scan for specific types of malware hidden in files and programs on the computer that the antivirus program was unable to catch.

Here are two links to the anti-malware software:
- https://www.malwarebytes.com/
- https://www.spybot-free-download.com/

5

Always keep the operating system updated to the latest version. MacOS and Windows are types of operating systems. These types of updates often include security patches that correct vulnerabilities that malware can exploit.


6

To reduce the chance of being exposed to ransomware attacks, it is advisable to take a regular backup of data. One way to do this can, for example, be to use OneDrive or Dropbox to store files in the cloud. If you are unlucky enough to be exposed to ransomware, you can only retrieve the files from the cloud.

Here is a link to programs you can use to save data in the cloud:
- https://www.microsoft.com/nb-no/microsoft-365/onedrive/download
- https://www.dropbox.com/install
